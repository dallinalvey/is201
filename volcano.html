  1. Ash from volcanoes can spark lightning

    Lightning commonly occurs in sync with volcanic eruptions. As ash rises, it builds static electricity similar to clouds, interacting with weather systems to trigger lightning strikes.

    • 75% of the world's volcanoes
    • The deadliest volcanoes in the United States, Japan, and the Philippines
    • Indonesia, the most volcanically active country on the Ring of Fire
